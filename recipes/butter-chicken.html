Delicious Homemade Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, is one of the most beloved dishes in Indian cuisine, cherished both in its homeland and across the globe. It features succulent pieces of chicken that are first marinated in a rich mixture of yogurt and an aromatic blend of spices, allowing the flavors to deeply penetrate the meat. The chicken is then cooked to perfection and enveloped in a velvety, tomato-based gravy that is infused with butter, cream, and a delicate balance of spices. The result is a dish that is simultaneously creamy, mildly spiced, and decadently flavorful, with every bite offering a perfect harmony of textures and tastes. Traditionally enjoyed with naan, roti, or steamed rice, butter chicken is more than just a meal—it’s a comforting culinary experience that evokes warmth, celebration, and the rich culinary heritage of North India. Whether served at a family dinner, a festive gathering, or a special occasion, this dish never fails to impress and satisfy.

Ingredients:

For marination:

  • 500g boneless chicken (cut into cubes)
  • 3 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Butter Chicken Gravy:

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil (optional, for cooking onions)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • 1/2 cup water (adjust for consistency)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

    • Optional Ingredients (for extra richness):

  • 1 tablespoon honey or sugar (to balance acidity of tomatoes)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (for color)
  • 1–2 green chilies, slit (for mild heat)

    • Steps for the Recipe:

    Step 1: Marinate the Chicken

    1. In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt.
    2. Add the chicken cubes and coat them evenly with the marinade.
    3. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1–2 hours, preferably overnight for maximum flavor.

    Step 2: Cook the Chicken

    1. Heat 1–2 tablespoons of butter in a pan over medium heat.
    2. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are almost cooked through (about 70–80%). They will finish cooking in the gravy.
    3. Remove the chicken and set aside.

    Step 3: Prepare the Gravy

    1. the same pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter (and a little oil if needed).
    2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    3. Add 2 teaspoons ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
    4. Pour in the tomato puree and cook for 5–7 minutes until the raw smell disappears and the mixture thickens.
    5. Add red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for another 2–3 minutes.

    Step 4: Combine Chicken and Gravy

    1. Add the partially cooked chicken to the gravy.
    2. Pour in ½ cup water (adjust for desired consistency).
    3. Cover and simmer on low heat for 10–15 minutes until the chicken is fully cooked and tender.

    Step 5: Finish the Dish

    1. Lower the heat and stir in ½ cup fresh cream.
    2. Crush kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle over the gravy.
    3. Add 1–2 tablespoons of butter on top for extra richness.
    4. Mix gently and simmer for another 2–3 minutes.

    Step 6: Garnish and Serve

    1. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
