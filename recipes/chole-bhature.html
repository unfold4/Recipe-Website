Delicious Homemade Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature" is a beloved dish from the northern regions of India, particularly Punjab, and has become a staple across the country in both homes and street food stalls. The name comes from two main components: “Chole”, meaning spiced chickpeas cooked in a tangy, aromatic gravy, and “Bhature”, a type of deep-fried, soft, and fluffy bread made from fermented dough. Traditionally, chole are simmered slowly with onions, tomatoes, ginger, garlic, and a unique blend of spices like cumin, coriander, and garam masala, giving them a hearty, flavorful, and slightly tangy taste. The bhature, when fried, puff up perfectly, providing a light and airy contrast to the robust curry. This combination creates a meal that is indulgent, filling, and incredibly satisfying. Chole Bhature has not only stood the test of time in Punjabi households but has also become a staple in Indian street food culture, often enjoyed during breakfast, brunch, or as a festive treat. Its popularity lies in the perfect balance of flavors and textures: the spiced, rich chickpeas paired with soft, golden fried bread. Every bite evokes warmth, celebration, and the vibrant culinary heritage of North India, making Chole Bhature a dish that’s as much about tradition as it is about flavor.

Ingredients:

For the Chole (Chickpea Curry):

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2–3 green chilies, slit (optional)
  • 2 teaspoons chole masala (or garam masala)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 2–3 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For the Bhature (Deep-Fried Bread):

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons semolina (sooji/rava) for crispiness (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder or 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • Water as needed to knead the dough
  • Oil for deep frying

Optional Ingredients / Enhancements:

  • 1 bay leaf or 2–3 black cardamoms for the chole gravy
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing) while tempering
  • Butter or ghee to drizzle over chole before serving

Steps for the Recipe:

Step 1: Prepare the Chickpeas

  1. If using dried chickpeas: Soak 1 cup chickpeas in enough water overnight (8–10 hours).
  2. Drain and rinse, then pressure cook with 3 cups water and a pinch of salt until soft (about 3–4 whistles).
  3. If using canned chickpeas: Drain, rinse, and set aside.

Step 2: Cook the Chole (Chickpea Curry)

  1. Heat 2–3 tablespoons oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add cumin seeds (and bay leaf/black cardamom if using). Let them sizzle.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies; sauté for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add pureed or chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until the oil separates from the masala (5–7 minutes).
  6. Add the cooked chickpeas and ½–1 cup of water (adjust for desired consistency).
  7. Stir in chole masala (or garam masala) and simmer for 10–15 minutes so the flavors meld.
  8. Add amchur powder or lemon juice for tanginess.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Step 3: Prepare the Bhature Dough

  1. In a bowl, combine 2 cups all-purpose flour, 2 tablespoons semolina (optional), 1 teaspoon sugar, ½ teaspoon salt, and ½ teaspoon baking powder (or yeast).
  2. Add 2 tablespoons yogurt and mix. Gradually add water and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1–2 hours (or 30–45 minutes if using baking powder instead of yeast).

Step 4: Shape and Fry Bhature

  1. Divide the dough into equal-sized balls.
  2. Roll each ball into a circle, about 5–6 inches in diameter.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat.
  4. Fry each bhatura one by one until golden brown and puffed (about 1–2 minutes per side).
  5. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.

Step 5: Serve

  1. Serve hot chole alongside fluffy bhature.
  2. Optional: Top chole with a dollop of butter or ghee for extra richness.
  3. Enjoy with pickles, sliced onions, or yogurt on the side.
