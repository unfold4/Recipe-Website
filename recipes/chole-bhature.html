Delicious Homemade Chole Bhature

Paneer Lababdar" is an indulgent North Indian curry that celebrates the richness of paneer in its most decadent form. The word "Lababdar" comes from Persian roots, meaning ‘desirable’ or ‘something that melts your heart with indulgence’. True to its name, this dish offers a luxurious experience—soft cubes of paneer simmered in a velvety gravy made with tomatoes, onions, butter, cream, and an aromatic blend of spices. What makes Paneer Lababdar unique is its dual-paneer texture: soft paneer cubes combined with a bit of grated paneer in the gravy, creating an extra creamy and slightly chunky consistency. Cashew paste adds a mild sweetness and a nutty depth, while fresh cream and butter give it a silky finish. The spices are well-balanced—mild enough to let the creamy flavors shine, yet aromatic enough to deliver that signature North Indian richness. Paneer Lababdar is a restaurant-style favorite, often served in weddings, festive feasts, and family dinners, paired with butter naan, roti, or fragrant jeera rice. Its vibrant orange color, rich texture, and irresistible aroma make it not just a meal, but an experience that evokes the grandeur of Mughlai cuisine blended with Punjabi warmth.

Ingredients:

250–300g paneer (cubed)

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1–2 tablespoons oil (optional)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 large tomatoes, pureed
  • 10–12 cashews (soaked in warm water for 15 minutes and ground into a paste)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1–2 green chilies, slit (optional, for mild heat)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1 tablespoon sugar or honey (optional, to balance acidity)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

    • Optional Ingredients (for extra richness):

  • 2 tablespoons grated paneer (to stir into the gravy for creamier texture)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (for color)

    • Steps for the Recipe:

    Step 1: Prepare Cashew Paste

    1. Soak 10–12 cashews in warm water for 15 minutes.
    2. Grind them into a smooth paste and keep aside.

    Step 2: Sauté Onions

    1. Heat 2 tablespoons butter (and oil, if using) in a pan over medium heat.
    2. Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

    Step 3: Add Ginger-Garlic and Tomatoes

    1. Add 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste and sauté for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
    2. Pour in the tomato puree and cook for 5–7 minutes until the raw smell disappears and the mixture thickens.

    Step 4: Add Spices

    1. Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt.
    2. Cook for 2–3 minutes to let the spices bloom.

    Step 5: Make the Gravy Creamy

    1. Add the cashew paste and stir well.
    2. Pour in ½ cup fresh cream and 1 tablespoon sugar (if using).
    3. Mix and simmer on low heat for 3–4 minutes until the gravy becomes rich and creamy.

    Step 6: Add Paneer

    1. Gently add the paneer cubes (and grated paneer, if using) into the gravy.
    2. Sprinkle slit green chilies, kasuri methi, and garam masala.
    3. Cover and simmer for 5–7 minutes on low heat so paneer absorbs the flavors.

    Step 7: Garnish and Serve

    1. Add a small knob of butter on top for extra richness.
    2. Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
    3. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
